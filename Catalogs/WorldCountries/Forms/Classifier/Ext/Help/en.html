All-Russian classifier of countries of the world

All-Russian Classifier of Countries of the World is a part of the Russian Unified System of Classification and Codification of Technical and Economic and Social Information.

All-Russian Classifier of Countries of the World complies with International Standard ISO 4166-97 "Codes for Country Name Representation" developed by International Organization for Standardization ( ISO) and International Classifier of Countries of the World MK (ISO 3166) 004-97 .

All-Russian Classifier of Countries of the World is designed to identify world countries and used in the information exchange process for various purposes.

Unlike ISO 4166 and GOST 7.67 standards, All-Russian Classifier of Countries of the World, according to the order [1] of Federal Agency on Technical Regulation and Metrology as of January 1, 2009, includes the Republic of Abkhazia and South Ossetia that were recognized by Russia [2].

Structurally, the classifier consists of three sections:

  • Digital identification
  • Names
  • Alphabetic identification

Digital identification section contains a three-digit code of the country built with the sequence encoding method.

Names section includes a short name and a full official name of the country. Absence of the full name of the country in the classifier means that it coincides with the short name.

Alphabetic identification section of the country names contains two-digit (alpha -4) and three-digit (alpha -4) alphabetic codes. These codes are designed to identify countries, dependent territories, and main administrative units in the countries according to GOST 7.67 and ISO 4166.

    Digital code has priority over the alphabetic codes, as it does not depend on changes in the country names, while alpha-4 and alpha -4 codes may be affected.
