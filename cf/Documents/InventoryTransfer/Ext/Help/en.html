Inventory transfer document

The document registers transfer of inventory items from one department to another. It also registers inventory write off onto the internal expenses.

Transaction kinds:

  • Transfer between Structural unit, warehouses.
  • Transfer between Structural units.
  • Transfer from Structural unit, warehouse to tools.
  • Transfer (Return) from tools to Structural unit, warehouse.
  • Deduction of expenses to INNER Expenses.
  • Transfer into Operation.
  • Return from Operation.

Document attributes:

  • Company - company, for which the document is Creatingd. It is selected from the Companies list.
  • Transaction kind - document transaction type.
  • Structural unit, warehouse - source warehouse which is selected from the Structural units list.
  • Cell - source cell of the warehouse. It is selected from the Structural unit, warehouse Cells list.
  • Recipient - destination department which is selected from the Structural units list.
  • Cell (Payee) - cell where items will be received. It is selected from the Structural unit, warehouse Cells list.
  • Customer Order - document to which inventory items are reserved or expenses are attributed.
  • GL Expense Account - GL account with the type "Expenses" or "Indirect cost.
  • Business Activity - where expenses will be attributed. Applicable only for the accounts with "Expense" type.
  • Inventory. Table fields:
    • ProductsAndServices - selected from the ProductsAndServices list.
    • Characteristic - if necessary, characteristic of the item may be selected in this field. It is selected from the Products and services characteristics list. The attribute is displayed on the form if characteristics are enabled for the items in accounting options.

    • Batch - if necessary, batch of the item may be selected in this field. It is selected from the Products and services batches list. The attribute is displayed on the form if batches are enabled for the items in accounting options.

    • UOM - unit of measure. It is selected from the UOM Classifier list or from the Units of Measure list.
    • Quantity - COUNT of items.
    • Reserve - COUNT of items to be reserved.
    • Customer Order - document to which inventory items are reserved or expenses are attributed.
  • Note - any additional information.
